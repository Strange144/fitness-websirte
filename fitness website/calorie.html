To calculate the daily calories, you need to determine the Total Daily
Energy Expenditure (TDEE) of an individual. TDEE represents the total
number of calories a person needs to maintain their current weight
based on various factors such as age, gender, weight, height, activity level,
and weight goal. There are several equations used to estimate TDEE, but one of the
commonly used formulas is the Harris-Benedict equation. The Harris-Benedict equation
has different versions for men and women:

For men:

TDEE = 88.362 + (13.397 × weight in kg) + (4.799 × height in cm) - (5.677 × age in years)

For women:

TDEE = 447.593 + (9.247 × weight in kg) + (3.098 × height in cm) - (4.330 × age in years)

After calculating TDEE, you need to consider the individual's activity level to adjust the calorie intake based on their lifestyle:

  1. Sedentary (little to no exercise): TDEE × 1.2
  2. Lightly active (light exercise/sports 1-3 days/week): TDEE × 1.375
  3. Moderately active (moderate exercise/sports 3-5 days/week): TDEE × 1.55
  4. Very active (hard exercise/sports 6-7 days a week): TDEE × 1.725
  5. Extra active (very hard exercise/sports, physical job, or training twice a day): TDEE × 1.9
